What I do

I am a PhD student in the Cognitive and Information Sciences department at University of California Merced

Introduction

I study emergence of social inequality, how inequality influences human behaviors, and belief dynamics in society. The tools that I deploy include computational modeling, such as dynamical system and agent-based models, and observational data of the real-world. I completed my Master's program in computer science at University of Souther California with a research project on utilizing machine learning with multimodal behavioral data to facilitate the annotation of utterances in psychotherapy sessions.
